Birds

While there is always something interesting to observe, you will find the greatest numbers of birds at the refuge from early November to late January. In the spring and fall, migratory bird species are moving through the refuge, resulting in high numbers of species.

  1. Golden Eagle

    Aquila chysaetos

    2. INFORMATION:

    The golden eagle (Aquila chrysaetos) is a bird of prey that lives in the Northern Hemisphere. It is the most widespread species of eagle. Golden eagles are North America's largest predatory bird. They are dark brown with long, broad wings. Their length ranges from 70 to 84 cm, and their wingspan ranges from 185 to 220 cm. Females are much larger than males.

  2. Burrowing Owl

    Athene cuninularia

    3. INFORMATION:

    These small, long-legged owls are found in open landscapes across North and South America. They live in areas with low vegetation, such as grasslands, deserts, and agricultural fields. They nest in underground burrows, often in abandoned mammal burrows.

  3. Chestnut-collared Longspur

    Calcarius ornatus

    4. INFORMATION:

    The chestnut-collared longspur (Calcarius ornatus) is a small, ground-feeding bird that primarily eats seeds. They are a member of the Calcariidae family. They breed in prairie habitats in Canada and the northern United States, and winter in the United States and Mexico.
